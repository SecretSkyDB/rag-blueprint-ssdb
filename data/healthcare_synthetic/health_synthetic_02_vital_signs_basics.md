# Synthetic vital signs reference (demonstration only)

**Note:** Simplified, fictional training text for retrieval demos. Not a clinical standard.

## Heart rate (pulse)

Resting heart rate in adults is commonly discussed between about 60 and 100 beats per minute. Athletes may have lower resting values. A sudden sustained increase with dizziness or chest discomfort should be evaluated by a professional.

## Blood pressure (illustrative ranges)

- Example labels used in public education materials: “normal” often refers to values below roughly 120/80 mmHg in many guidelines; “elevated” and “hypertension” categories depend on repeated measurements and context. Always interpret readings with a clinician.
- Home monitors should be validated devices; cuff size and arm position matter.

## Respiratory rate

At rest, roughly 12–20 breaths per minute is a frequently cited range for adults. Rapid breathing with fever or low oxygen symptoms needs timely assessment.

## Body temperature

Oral temperature around 37°C (98.6°F) is a classic “average” reference, but circadian and individual variation is normal. Fever protocols differ by age group and comorbidities.
