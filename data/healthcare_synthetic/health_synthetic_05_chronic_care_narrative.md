# Synthetic chronic care follow-up (demonstration)

**Fictional narrative for RAG testing — not a real patient.**

## Case summary (composite)

**Demographics (synthetic):** 58-year-old with Type 2 diabetes and mild hypertension, managed in primary care.

**Goals of care (illustrative):** Maintain HbA1c in a range agreed with the care team, reduce hypoglycemia events, and protect kidney function. Blood pressure targets are individualized; home readings are compared to clinic cuff measurements for consistency.

**Self-management:** Uses a mobile log for meals and walk minutes. Reports walking about 120 minutes per week. Nutrition counseling emphasized fiber-rich foods and portion awareness rather than a named fad diet.

**Red flags the patient was told to report early:** new foot wounds, vision changes, persistent nausea, or repeated low glucose readings on meter.

**Care coordination (fictional):** A quarterly phone check with a diabetes educator and annual eye and foot screening were documented in the synthetic chart note used for this demo.
