# Synthetic telehealth FAQ (demonstration only)

**Note:** Fictional content for RAG / demo testing. Not medical advice.

## What is a telehealth visit?

A telehealth visit is a remote appointment with a licensed clinician using secure video or phone. It can be used for follow-up care, minor symptom review, medication reconciliation, and some screening discussions when in-person exam is not required.

## How to prepare

- List current medications (name, dose, frequency) and any allergies.
- Have your pharmacy name and a reliable phone number for callbacks.
- Find a private, well-lit area; use headphones if you share a space.
- If monitoring devices are available, note recent blood pressure or glucose readings the clinician asked you to track.

## When to seek urgent in-person or emergency care

- Chest pain, sudden weakness on one side, or difficulty speaking.
- Severe shortness of breath, fainting, or uncontrolled bleeding.
- High fever with confusion, stiff neck, or severe headache “worst in your life.”

## Technical tips

- Test camera and microphone a few minutes before the scheduled time.
- If the call drops, use the phone number in your appointment link for a voice backup.
